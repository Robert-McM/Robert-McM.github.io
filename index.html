about

Robert McManus is a biologist who partners with researchers and academics to generate reproducible and publishable data. By understanding the underlying framework of statistical analysis, Robert believes that a simple, yet elegant answer is the key to argue the validity of a hypothesis effectively. Robert designed a study of NGS data centered on population structure and another primary research experiment measuring changes in gene expression using qPCR. Robert has over one year of research experience as an intern and in supervised research, while all labs have remained in person and include hands-on experience collecting actual data. In addition to his research experience, Robert has two years of experience as a medical scribe and trainer and currently works as a restaurant server. Robert will graduate Summa Cum Laude from Texas Wesleyan University with departmental honors holding a 3.95 GPA in spring 2021.
